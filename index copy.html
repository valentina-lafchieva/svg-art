What Are SVG Objects?

SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) are XML-based vector images used to define two-dimensional graphics in web design. Unlike raster images, SVGs are resolution-independent, meaning they look crisp at any size or resolution. They can be used to create intricate shapes, designs, and animations that scale across devices without losing quality.

In this part of the virtual museum, you’ll experience the beauty of SVGs with dynamic parallax effects that give the images depth and movement.

What is the meaning of SVG Logos?

SVG logos are digital graphics created using mathematical equations and geometric shapes, allowing them to scale infinitely without losing quality. Unlike JPEG or PNG, they are lightweight, support transparency, and can be easily animated or styled with CSS, making them perfect for responsive web design and modern branding.

What is SVG Art?

SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) is a web standard for creating resolution-independent graphics. Unlike raster images, SVGs use mathematical paths to define shapes, lines, and colors, making them perfect for high-quality visuals that look sharp at any size. Tools like Adobe Illustrator, Inkscape, and Figma allow artists to create SVG art effortlessly. Whether it’s logos, illustrations, or animations, SVGs are a cornerstone of modern web design and digital art.
